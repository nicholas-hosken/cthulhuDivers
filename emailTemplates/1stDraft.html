Musa

The most popular dive site in Cancun, perhaps Mexico, has got to be Cancun's Underwater Museum which is positioned off the south eastern coast of Isla Mujeres. Constructed by British artist Jason deCaires Taylor, it was built to relieve some of the pressure from all the visitors on the surrounding reefs. These life-size sculptures have all been allowed to have algae grow on them. This algae then, in turn, facilitates the growth of actual coral on each figure. In fact, when visiting these manmade sculptures, you will see how this coral has started to take hold and is now slowly transforming the sculptures into a very unique artificial reef.
 

Manchones Grande

Just a short two minute boat ride from MUSA, we find the stunning Manchones Reef system which is one of our healthiest reefs in the Mexican state of Quintana Roo. It owes its health to its shallow depth (approximately 9m/30ft) and the crystal clear water in which it finds itself. These two characteristics allow for a large amount of sunlight to hit the reef which means photosynthesis can easily occur in the algae found here. And when specific algae is able to thrive, coral heads are able to flourish and the entire local ecosystem begins to bloom leading to a large schools of fish and other sea life moving in.
